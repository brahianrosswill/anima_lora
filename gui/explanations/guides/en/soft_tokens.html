Soft Tokens

Per-layer, time-indexed soft text tokens in the SoftREPA parameterization (Lee et al., "Aligning Text to Image in Diffusion Models is Easier Than You Think", NeurIPS 2025, arXiv:2503.08250). The DiT is frozen — the only trainable parameters are a small bank of learned token embeddings (~1M params) spliced into the cross-attention text stream. Each of the leading n_layers blocks gets K soft tokens, and the bank is indexed by a t-bucket (σ ∈ [0,1] split into n_t_buckets), so the steering is both per-layer and per-timestep.

Knobs: network_dim → K (tokens per layer per bucket); n_layers (leading DiT blocks to attach to — Anima has 28; SoftREPA used 5/24 on SD3, scale proportionally); n_t_buckets (under-sampled buckets just stay near zero, not catastrophic); splice_position — end_of_sequence (default) overwrites the K tail zero-pad slots to preserve the strongest attention sinks, or front_of_padding scatters per-sample at each caption's end (position-aware).

We adopt only the parameterization and skip SoftREPA's contrastive InfoNCE objective — training runs under the standard flow-matching loss. An optional contrastive objective (cached-TE negatives) is wired but off by default (contrastive_weight=0.0 is bit-identical to plain FM); each negative costs an extra full DiT forward, so keep contrastive_k at 1–2. See docs/proposal/soft_tokens_contrastive.md.

Train with make exp-soft-tokens; the soft tokens are spliced at inference too (make exp-test-soft / ComfyUI Anima Adapter Loader). Config: configs/methods/soft_tokens.toml.
